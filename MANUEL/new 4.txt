    <h2>Common Text Formatting Tags</h2>
    <p>HTML provides several tags to format text for emphasis, style, or structure. Here’s a summary:</p>

    <ul>
        <li><strong><b></strong> – Bold text. <em>Use:</em> Highlight important words.</li>
        <li><strong><strong></strong> – Important text, usually bold. <em>Use:</em> Emphasize meaning, accessible for screen readers.</li>
        <li><strong><i></strong> – Italic text. <em>Use:</em> Highlight terms, foreign words, or technical terms.</li>
        <li><strong><em></strong> – Emphasized text, usually italic. <em>Use:</em> Convey stress or importance in a sentence.</li>
        <li><strong><mark></strong> – Highlighted text. <em>Use:</em> Draw attention to specific parts of content.</li>
        <li><strong><small></strong> – Smaller text. <em>Use:</em> For fine print or secondary information.</li>
        <li><strong><del></strong> – Strikethrough text. <em>Use:</em> Indicate removed or outdated content.</li>
        <li><strong><ins></strong> – Inserted text, usually underlined. <em>Use:</em> Show new or added content.</li>
        <li><strong><sub></strong> – Subscript text. <em>Use:</em> Chemical formulas, math indices.</li>
        <li><strong><sup></strong> – Superscript text. <em>Use:</em> Exponents, footnotes, ordinal numbers.</li>
    </ul>
</div>
